The concept was first introduced by biologist Garrett Hardin in his 1968 essay of the same name. The case study revolves around a pasture that is shared by a group of herdsmen who are free to use the land as they see fit. This leads to a situation where the pasture is overgrazed, and the herdsmen suffer as a result. The case study highlights the importance of managing shared resources through collective action or the establishment of rules and regulations to prevent overuse and ensure the long-term sustainability of the resource.
