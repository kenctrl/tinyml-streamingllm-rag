2. Endothermic Reactions:
Endothermic reactions are chemical reactions that absorb heat and energy from the environment. In these reactions, the enthalpy change is positive, meaning that the reaction absorbs heat from the surroundings. The energy absorbed during an endothermic reaction can be in the form of light, sound, or heat.

Criteria:

* Heat is absorbed from the environment
* Enthalpy change is positive
* Energy is transferred from the surroundings to the reactants

Example:
The melting of ice is an example of an endothermic reaction. The reaction absorbs heat from the surroundings, and the enthalpy change is positive.

In summary, exothermic reactions release heat and energy into the environment, while endothermic reactions absorb heat and energy from the environment. The criteria used to distinguish between the two types of reactions include the direction of enthalpy change and the transfer of energy between the reactants and the surroundings.
