ASSISTANT: 1. GDP (Gross Domestic Product): GDP is a measure of the total value of goods and services produced within a country's borders over a specific period of time. It is considered a key indicator of a country's economic health and growth.
2. Inflation: Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising, leading to a decrease in the purchasing power of a currency. It is typically measured as an annual percentage increase.
3. Unemployment Rate: The unemployment rate is the percentage of the labor force that is unemployed but actively seeking employment. It is often used as a measure of the health of the labor market and the overall economy.

Fiscal Policy: Fiscal policy refers to the use of government spending and taxation to influence the economy. Expansionary fiscal policy involves increasing government spending and/or reducing taxes to stimulate economic growth, while contractionary fiscal policy involves decreasing government spending and/or increasing taxes to reduce inflation and control government debt.

Monetary Policy: Monetary policy refers to the actions taken by central banks to influence the money supply and interest rates in order to control inflation and stabilize the economy. Expansionary monetary policy involves increasing the money supply and lowering interest rates to stimulate economic growth, while contractionary monetary policy involves decreasing the money supply and raising interest rates to reduce inflation and control inflation.

The relationship between these indicators is complex and can vary depending on the state of the economy. For example, during a recession, unemployment rates may rise while inflation rates may decrease. In contrast, during an economic boom, GDP may increase while inflation rates may rise.

Fiscal and monetary policies can have a significant impact on these indicators. Expansionary fiscal and monetary policies can stimulate economic growth and increase employment, but they can also lead to inflation and higher interest rates. Contractionary fiscal and monetary policies can reduce inflation and control government debt, but they can also lead to lower economic growth and higher unemployment rates.

In conclusion, understanding the relationship between economic indicators and the impact of fiscal and monetary policies is essential for policymakers and investors to make informed decisions about the economy.

Antitrust laws are a set of regulations designed to promote fair competition in markets and prevent monopolies or other anti-competitive practices. These laws aim to protect consumers from price-fixing, collusion, and other practices that can harm competition and lead to higher prices.

In the United States, antitrust laws are enforced by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) and the Department of Justice (DOJ). The two main federal antitrust statutes are the Sherman Act and the Clayton Act. These laws prohibit anticompetitive practices such as price-fixing, bid-rigging, and mergers that reduce competition.

In China, antitrust laws are enforced by the National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) and the State Administration for Market Regulation (SAMR). The two main antitrust laws in China are the Anti-Monopoly Law (AML) and the Interim Provisions on the Investigation of Cases of Monopolistic Agreements and Abuse of Dominant Market Position. These laws prohibit practices such as price-fixing, market allocation, and abuse of dominant market position.

One notable case study involving antitrust laws in the US is the Microsoft antitrust case. In 1998, the DOJ filed a lawsuit against Microsoft, alleging that the company had engaged in anticompetitive practices that harmed consumers and stifled innovation. The case was eventually settled in 2002, with Microsoft agreeing to a series of restrictions on its business practices.

In China, the antitrust case involving Qualcomm is a notable example. In 2015, the NDRC fined Qualcomm $975 million for abusing its dominant position in the market for wireless chips. Qualcomm appealed the decision, but in 2018, the Supreme People's Court upheld the fine.

In conclusion, antitrust laws play a crucial role in promoting fair competition and protecting consumers from anti-competitive practices. While the laws and regulations vary between countries, the goal of promoting a competitive market remains the same.

* Historical information about the Opium Wars
* Drama and mime props (e.g. fans, hats, robes, etc.)
* Whiteboards and markers

* Explain that students will be using drama and mime techniques to explore the events of the Opium Wars.

* Provide a brief overview of the Opium Wars, including the key events and players involved.

* Discuss how these techniques can be used to convey emotions and ideas.
* Demonstrate how to use props to create different characters and settings.
1. Group Work (15 minutes)
* Divide students into small groups.
* Assign each group a specific event or aspect of the Opium Wars to explore through drama and mime.
* Provide time for students to create and rehearse their performances.

Day 2:

1. Performance (20 minutes)
* Allow each group to perform their drama or mime for the class.
* Encourage the class to provide feedback and ask questions about the performances.
1. Discussion (20 minutes)
* Lead a class discussion about the different performances and the historical events they represent.
* Discuss how the use of drama and mime techniques can help to bring history to life.
1. Reflection (15 minutes)
* Ask students to reflect on their experience using drama and mime techniques to explore history.
* Encourage them to think about how these techniques can be used in other historical contexts.

Day 3:

1. Review (10 minutes)
* Review the key events and concepts of the Opium Wars.
* Use whiteboards to create a summary of the conflict.
1. Creative Project (20 minutes)

1. Sharing (15 minutes)
* Allow students to share their creative projects with the class.

* Participation in group work and class discussions (20%)
* Quality of creative project (30%)
* Quality of performance (30%)
* Reflection on the use of drama and mime techniques (20%)
