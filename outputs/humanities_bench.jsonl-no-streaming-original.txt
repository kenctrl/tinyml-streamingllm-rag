Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is increasing. High inflation can lead to decreased purchasing power, as the same amount of money can buy fewer goods and services over time.

Unemployment rates measure the percentage of the labor force that is without jobs but actively seeking employment. High unemployment rates can indicate an economic downturn, as businesses may reduce their workforce or stop hiring altogether.

However, lower interest rates may also lead to higher inflation.

In summary, GDP, inflation, and unemployment rates are interconnected, and fiscal and monetary policies can influence these indicators. Understanding these relationships is crucial for policymakers and businesses to make informed decisions about economic policy and strategy.
